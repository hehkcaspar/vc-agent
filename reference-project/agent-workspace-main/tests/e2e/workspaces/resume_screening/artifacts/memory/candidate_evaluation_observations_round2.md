## Update to Previous Observations (Round 1)

This evaluation covered 8 candidates across 3 positions. Key new insights and patterns observed:

## New Patterns Observed in Round 2

### 1. **AI Tool Fluency is Now Table Stakes**
- All top 3 hires (吴少婷, 徐楚辉, 王雨) actively use AI frameworks daily
- Candidates mentioning LangChain, LLM APIs, n8n, Dify, Coze immediately stand out
- Traditional Python/web scraping without AI integration = low score (孙荣阳: 3/10)
- **Insight**: AI proficiency is no longer a "bonus" - it's the baseline requirement for technical roles

### 2. **Location Mismatch = Automatic Rejection**
- 钟李涛 (Shenzhen) and 孙荣阳 (Hangzhou) rejected solely on location
- Even strong technical skills cannot overcome Guangzhou requirement
- **Insight**: On-site collaboration is non-negotiable for this small team model

### 3. **Experience Level Precision Matters**
- 黄晨 (10 years, 15-20K salary) rejected for design role despite strong portfolio
- JD explicitly wants 0-2yr for growth/design role to avoid "固化 thinking"
- Overqualified candidates may resist fast-scratch culture
- **Insight**: Don't overhire - match experience level to role scope

### 4. **Quantifiable Impact Trumps Technical Lists**
- 吴少婷's resume stood out because of specific metrics:
  - "300% efficiency gain"
  - "95% automation rate"
  - "4 hours → 5 minutes processing time"
- Candidates listing technologies without business impact scored lower
- **Insight**: Numbers prove product ownership and result-orientation

### 5. **Independent Shipping Demonstrates Ownership**
- 徐楚辉's solo portalyze.ai project showed strong ownership mindset
- Candidates who built products end-to-end (not just components) ranked higher
- **Insight**: Look for evidence of 0-1 shipping, not just feature implementation

### 6. **English Ability Correlates with International Market Fit**
- Top candidates all have English credentials (CET-4, US education, TOEFL/GRE)
- Company targets US/European markets - English reading/writing essential
- **Insight**: Include English assessment in interview process

### 7. **PDF Portfolio Files Often Corrupted**
- 苏莉盈 and 陈诺韵's PDFs were unreadable (encrypted/corrupted)
- Design/growth candidates should provide live URLs or Google Drive links
- **Insight**: Request accessible portfolio formats upfront

## Revised Scoring Criteria (Updated from Round 1)

### Weighted Evaluation Framework:

| Criterion | Weight | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| AI Tool Proficiency | 25% | Must show active use (LangChain, LLM APIs, etc.) |
| Product Ownership/Shipped Work | 25% | Evidence of end-to-end delivery |
| Quantifiable Impact | 20% | Metrics showing business results |
| Location Alignment | 15% | Guangzhou mandatory |
| Experience Level Match | 10% | Not overqualified for role scope |
| English Ability | 5% | CET-4+ or equivalent |

### Score Interpretation:
- **9-10**: Exceptional match, priority hire
- **7-8.5**: Strong candidate, good hire
- **6-6.5**: Marginal fit, consider if pipeline weak
- **<6**: Clear mismatch, reject

## Position-Specific Insights

### Software Engineer (Full-stack/AI):
**Ideal Profile:**
- 0-3 years experience (recent grads to early career)
- Active AI tool usage documented in projects
- Shipped at least 1 production application
- Guangzhou-based or willing to relocate immediately
- English reading ability (CET-4+)

**Red Flags:**
- Location outside Guangzhou
- No AI tool mentions
- Enterprise-only background without startup exposure
- Overqualified (5+ years for entry-level role)

### Growth/Design Lead:
**Ideal Profile:**
- 0-2 years experience (fresh perspective preferred)
- Live portfolio with deployed pages/apps
- Frontend coding capability (not just Figma)
- Salary expectation within 9-14K range
- Growth/marketing data-driven mindset

**Red Flags:**
- 5+ years experience (cultural misalignment)
- Salary expectations >15K
- Design-only portfolio without code
- Traditional agency background

### Office Assistant:
**Status**: No candidates submitted in Round 2

**Ideal Profile:**
- Detail-oriented personality
- Willingness to learn AI office tools
- Basic English reading
- Startup environment adaptability
- Admin/HR operations interest

## Interview Question Bank (Updated)

### For Technical Roles:
1. "Walk me through your AI development workflow. What tools do you use daily?"
2. "Describe a product you shipped from 0-1. What was your role vs. the team's?"
3. "Give me a specific example where you improved efficiency with AI. What were the metrics?"
4. "How do you approach learning new tech stacks? Show me something you learned recently."
5. "What does 'vibe coding' mean to you? How do you practice it?"

### For Culture Fit:
1. "Why Causally? What appeals to you about an incubation studio model?"
2. "Describe a time you had to iterate quickly based on feedback. What did you learn?"
3. "How do you balance perfectionism with shipping speed?"
4. "What's your ideal work environment? Fast-paced startup or structured corporation?"
5. "Where do you see yourself in 2 years? How does this role fit that vision?"

### For English Assessment:
1. "Read this technical article excerpt aloud and summarize key points"
2. "Explain your most complex project in English"
3. "How comfortable are you reading English tech documentation daily?"

## Lessons Learned Summary

### What Works:
1. ✅ **AI tool mentions = immediate attention**
2. ✅ **Quantifiable metrics > technology lists**
3. ✅ **Live product URLs > theoretical projects**
4. ✅ **Guangzhou location is mandatory**
5. ✅ **Younger candidates (0-2yr) fit culture better**
6. ✅ **Independent shipping demonstrates ownership**
7. ✅ **English ability correlates with international market success**

### What Doesn't Work:
1. ❌ **Enterprise-only backgrounds** without startup exposure
2. ❌ **Overqualified candidates** resisting fast-scratch culture
3. ❌ **Design-only portfolios** without frontend shipping
4. ❌ **Location mismatches** (automatic rejection)
5. ❌ **Salary expectations above budget** (waste of time)
6. ❌ **Corrupted file formats** (request accessible alternatives)

### Critical Success Factors:
1. **AI-first mindset** - must already use AI tools daily
2. **Product ownership** - evidence of end-to-end delivery
3. **Startup agility** - comfort with uncertainty and rapid iteration
4. **Right experience level** - not overqualified for role scope
5. **Location alignment** - Guangzhou mandatory for on-site collaboration
